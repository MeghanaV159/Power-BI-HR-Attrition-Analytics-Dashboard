# 📊 HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard

## 🧠 Project Overview
Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it leads to increased hiring costs, loss of experienced talent, and reduced productivity. This project analyzes employee data and builds an interactive Power BI dashboard to identify key factors driving attrition and help HR teams make informed decisions.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze employee attrition trends  
- Identify key drivers of employee turnover  
- Build an interactive dashboard for HR insights  
- Develop a risk model to predict attrition probability  

---

## 🚀 What’s New / Innovation
- Built a custom Attrition Risk Model to move beyond descriptive analytics  
- Classified employees into Low, Medium, and High Risk categories  
- Used key factors like work-life balance, job satisfaction, and overtime  
- Developed a risk scoring mechanism to measure attrition likelihood  
- Enabled early identification of high-risk employees for proactive action  
- Added a risk scorecard to compare work-life balance across risk levels  
- Shifted analysis from “what happened” to “what might happen”

## ⚠️ Risk Modeling
A risk model was created using:
- Work-life balance  
- Job satisfaction  
- Overtime  

### Risk Categories:
- Low Risk  
- Medium Risk  
- High Risk  

This helps HR teams proactively identify employees likely to leave.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Highest attrition observed in **26–35 age group**  
- Employees earning **below 5K salary** show maximum attrition  
- **Overtime employees** have significantly higher attrition rates  
- Poor **work-life balance** increases turnover  
- **Laboratory Technicians & Research Scientists** show higher exits  
- Medium & High-risk employees contribute most to attrition
